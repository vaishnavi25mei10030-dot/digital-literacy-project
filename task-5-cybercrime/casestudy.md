# Case Study of UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is one of the major online crimes taking place in India wherein the hacker lures the victim into transferring money or divulging sensitive bank-related information. Such types of online fraud are often perpetrated against students, elderly people, and those who are not sufficiently aware of online payment security.

The general scenario of this type of online fraud is that the hacker poses himself as a bank employee or customer service representative. He contacts the victim either through a phone call, message, or a fake website and tells him that there is some issue with his bank account or that he has received a reward. He asks the victim to click on a suspicious link or disclose the OTP (One-Time Password) details. In some instances, the hacker also sends a “collect request” to the victim through the UPI application, which the victim mistakenly approves, leading to a transfer of money from the victim’s account.

The victims of this type of online fraud are people who are often indulging in online transactions but are not sufficiently aware of online scams. Students are also a major target group for this type of online fraud.

The impact of this type of online fraud is that the victim faces financial loss and mental stress. In some instances, the victim also loses a considerable amount of money and faces difficulties in retrieving it.
